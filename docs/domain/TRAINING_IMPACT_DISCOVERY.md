# Training Impact — Domain Discovery

> A domain discovery document. Not a model. Not a spec.
> Its only purpose: to understand what it means for a workout to have an impact on an athlete.

---

## The central distinction

**A workout is an event. Impact is a consequence.**

A workout is something that happened: a duration, an effort, a set of measurements. It is finite, recorded, in the past.

Impact is what the workout *did*. It unfolds after the workout ends — in the hours, days, weeks, and sometimes months that follow. It is not recorded by any device. It is not in the data.

Aurora does not study workouts. Aurora studies consequences.

This is the hardest thing about the domain: **the thing Aurora cares about is never directly observable.** We see the event. We must infer the consequence.

---

## How to read this document

Every substantive statement is tagged. Aurora must stay intellectually honest about what it knows versus what it believes.

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[FACT]** | Well-established in exercise physiology or self-evidently true. |
| **[HYPOTHESIS]** | Plausible and reasoned, but unproven for our athletes. Must be validated. |
| **[ASSUMPTION]** | A choice Aurora makes to move forward. Not a truth — a stance. |
| **[QUESTION]** | An open question we must answer before designing. |
| **[UNKNOWN]** | We genuinely don't know, and we should not pretend otherwise. |

If a statement is wrong, the tag tells us how much is at stake in being wrong.

## Part 1 — The dimensions of impact

A workout does not have *an* impact. It has impacts, across different dimensions, on different timescales, with different certainty. Discovering them separately is the first step.

---

### 1.1 Physical Impact

How a workout changes the body.

This is the most studied dimension and the one Aurora can reason about with the most confidence — but also the one where it's easiest to mistake the measurable for the meaningful.

**Concepts:**

- **Adaptation** — The body becomes more capable in response to a stimulus it was not prepared for. This is the entire point of training. [FACT] Adaptation is real and is the mechanism by which athletes improve.

- **Accumulated Fatigue** — Residual cost of training that has not yet been recovered. It suppresses performance temporarily and, if unmanaged, indefinitely. [FACT]

- **Recovery Demand** — The amount and kind of rest a workout requires before its adaptation can be realized. [FACT] that recovery is required; [HYPOTHESIS] that Aurora can estimate the *demand* of a given workout for a given athlete.

- **Endurance** — The capacity to sustain effort over time. A workout can extend it. [FACT]

- **Speed / Power** — The capacity to produce high output. A workout can sharpen it. [FACT]

- **Strength** — The capacity to produce force. [FACT]

- **Mobility / Durability** — The capacity of the body to tolerate the mechanical demands of the sport without breaking down. [FACT] that it matters; [UNKNOWN] whether Aurora can observe it from training data at all.

**The critical insight:** [FACT] Adaptation does not happen during the workout. It happens during recovery, *after* the stimulus. The workout creates a demand; the body answers it later. This means physical impact is, by nature, delayed — and the same workout produces no adaptation at all if recovery never comes.

---

### 1.2 Technical Impact

How a workout changes execution — the quality of *how* the athlete moves.

This dimension is almost entirely invisible to the data Aurora currently reasons about, which makes it both important and humbling.

**Concepts:**

- **Efficiency** — Producing the same output for less cost (metabolic, mechanical). An efficient athlete goes faster at the same effort, or the same speed at less effort. [FACT] efficiency matters and improves with practice.

- **Movement Quality** — Stroke mechanics (swimming), pedaling smoothness (cycling), running form. [FACT] it matters. [UNKNOWN] whether it can be inferred from the signals Aurora has, or whether it requires observation Aurora does not have access to.

- **Pacing** — The skill of distributing effort across a session or race. [FACT] it is a learned skill and a major determinant of outcomes. [HYPOTHESIS] Aurora can observe pacing decisions in the data and learn whether the athlete is improving at them.

- **Coordination / Skill** — Sport-specific motor learning (open-water sighting, cornering, transitions). [FACT] it is trained; [UNKNOWN] whether training data reveals it.

**The critical insight:** [HYPOTHESIS] Technical impact may be the dimension where Aurora is weakest, because technique is mostly invisible to sensors. Aurora must be honest that a session can produce enormous technical impact (a breakthrough in form) that leaves almost no signature in the numbers. **[QUESTION] Should Aurora attempt to reason about technical impact at all, or explicitly declare it out of scope and rely on the athlete to report it?**

---

### 1.3 Mental Impact

What a workout changes psychologically.

The thesis claims that "the decisions sustained under pressure shape the athlete." If that is true, the mental dimension is not secondary — it may be the most durable form of impact a workout has.

**Concepts — and why each matters:**

- **Confidence** — Belief in one's own capability. It matters because [HYPOTHESIS] confidence changes what an athlete is willing to attempt, and attempting harder things is how athletes grow. A workout that proves "I can do this" may have more lasting impact than its physical adaptation.

- **Tolerance to Discomfort** — The trained ability to stay with hard effort. It matters because [FACT] in endurance sport, the limiter at the decisive moment is frequently the willingness to suffer, not the physiology. Workouts train this directly.

- **Discipline / Consistency** — The capacity to show up, especially when motivation is absent. It matters because [HYPOTHESIS] consistency over time is the single strongest predictor of long-term improvement — more than the brilliance of any individual session.

- **Commitment** — The depth of the athlete's investment in the goal. It matters because it sustains everything else through difficulty.

- **Ability to Sustain Decisions Under Pressure** — The thesis's core claim about character. It matters because [ASSUMPTION] this is what Aurora believes ultimately separates athletes who reach their potential from those who don't.

**The critical insight:** [UNKNOWN] Almost none of this is directly measurable from training data. [HYPOTHESIS] Some of it may be *indirectly* inferable — a pattern of consistency is visible over time; a workout completed despite signals of high fatigue may indicate something about tolerance. But the inference is fragile and Aurora must not overstate it. **[QUESTION] Can mental impact be inferred from behavior over time (showing up, finishing, choosing the hard option), even if it cannot be measured in a single session?**

---

### 1.4 Competitive Impact

How today's training affects future competition.

This is where impact becomes *purposeful*. The athlete does not train in the abstract; they train toward something.

**Concepts:**

- **Race Readiness** — The convergence of fitness, freshness, and skill at a target moment. [FACT] it is real and is the goal of periodized training. [HYPOTHESIS] Aurora can estimate it.

- **Event Specificity** — How closely a workout's demands match the demands of the target competition. A flat workout prepares poorly for a mountain race. [FACT] specificity matters; the closer to competition, the more it matters.

- **Tactical Execution** — The ability to make good decisions during competition (when to push, when to hold). [FACT] it is trainable; [UNKNOWN] whether training data reveals progress in it.

- **Confidence Under Pressure** — The competitive expression of the mental dimension. [HYPOTHESIS] certain workouts build it specifically (simulation, rehearsal of race conditions).

**The critical insight:** [FACT] The competitive impact of a workout depends entirely on *what it is preparing for*. The same workout is excellent preparation for one race and irrelevant for another. **Impact cannot be assessed without knowing the target.** This means competitive impact is contextual in a way physical impact is not.

---

### 1.5 Longitudinal Impact

The dimension of time. Some workouts do almost nothing today and prove decisive months later. Others feel enormous today and leave nothing behind.

This is the hardest and most important section, because it is where Aurora's thesis ("transformation is longitudinal") meets the domain.

**Three modes of impact across time:**

- **Immediate Impact** — Effect visible within hours to a few days. Acute fatigue is immediate. A confidence boost is immediate. [FACT] some impact is immediate and observable soon after the event.

- **Delayed Impact** — Effect that appears only after a lag. The classic case: [FACT] adaptation lags the stimulus by days to weeks (supercompensation). The workout's benefit is invisible at first and emerges later. A hard block can *lower* performance for two weeks and then produce a peak.

- **Accumulated Impact** — Effect that no single workout produces, but that many workouts produce together. [FACT] Aerobic base, durability, and consistency are built by accumulation, not by any individual session. The impact belongs to the pattern, not the event.

**How does Aurora distinguish them?**

[HYPOTHESIS] Immediate impact can be estimated from the workout and the athlete's near-term state. [HYPOTHESIS] Delayed impact can only be confirmed in hindsight, by observing what the athlete became — which means Aurora's understanding of a workout *should change over time as more is known*. A workout's true impact may only be assessable weeks after it happened.

[ASSUMPTION] This implies something important about Aurora's nature: **Aurora's understanding of a workout is not final when the workout ends. It matures.** The judgment Aurora makes today is provisional. This is a stance, not a fact — but it follows directly from the longitudinal thesis.

**The critical insight:** [HYPOTHESIS] The workouts with the highest long-term impact may be precisely the ones with the lowest immediate signal — the unremarkable consistency that builds a base. If true, this is dangerous: a system that rewards immediate, measurable impact would systematically undervalue the workouts that matter most. **[QUESTION] How does Aurora avoid over-weighting dramatic sessions and under-weighting quiet, accumulative ones?**

## Part 2 — The relationship between impacts

The mission proposes this chain:

```
Training → Physical Impact → Athlete State → Future Training Decisions → Long-Term Trajectory
```

We were asked not to assume it is correct. It is not. It is a useful first draft with three serious problems.

**Problem 1 — It collapses all impact into "physical."**
The chain only routes through physical impact. But mental, technical, and competitive impacts also feed athlete state and decisions. A workout that builds confidence changes future decisions without changing physiology. The chain is too narrow.

**Problem 2 — It is linear and one-directional. The domain is a loop.**
"Athlete State → Decisions → Trajectory" is drawn as an arrow ending in trajectory. But trajectory feeds back: who the athlete is becoming changes what training they should do next, which changes the impact of that training. [FACT] Training is a feedback loop, not a pipeline.

**Problem 3 — It omits the athlete's individuality, which is where impact is actually determined.**
The chain treats "Training → Physical Impact" as if the workout determines the impact. It does not. The *athlete* determines the impact. The same training produces different impact in different bodies. The arrow from Training to Impact is mediated by who the athlete is.

**A more honest model:**

```
                    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
                    │                                            │
                    ▼                                            │
   WORKOUT ──(mediated by)──► ATHLETE ──produces──► IMPACT       │
   (event)        ▲          (the      across       (consequence)│
                  │           mediator) dimensions:      │       │
                  │                      physical        │       │
                  │                      technical       │       │
                  │                      mental          ▼       │
                  │                      competitive  CHANGED    │
                  │                                    ATHLETE    │
                  │                                    STATE      │
                  │                                       │       │
                  │                                       ▼       │
                  │                              FUTURE DECISIONS │
                  │                                       │       │
                  └───────────────shapes──────────────────┘       │
                                                          │       │
                                                          ▼       │
                                                  LONG-TERM ──────┘
                                                  TRAJECTORY
                                                  (who the athlete
                                                   is becoming)
```

The essential corrections:
1. **The athlete sits between the workout and the impact.** [FACT] The workout is an input; the athlete is the function; the impact is the output. Change the function and the same input yields a different output.
2. **Impact is multidimensional**, not just physical.
3. **It is a loop.** Trajectory feeds back into decisions and into how the athlete responds to the next stimulus.
4. **Aurora's job is to understand the mediator** — the athlete — well enough to infer the consequence from the event.

## Part 3 — The success criterion

> **Why can two athletes perform exactly the same workout and experience completely different impacts?**

If Part 2 is right, the answer is structural, not incidental. The same workout produces different impact because **the workout is only the input; the athlete is what turns input into consequence.** Two different functions, given the same input, return different outputs.

Concretely, the athletes differ in:

- **Current state** — [FACT] A workout lands on a fatigued athlete differently than on a fresh one. Identical sessions, different starting points, different consequences (one adapts, one digs deeper into a hole).

- **Training history** — [FACT] The same stimulus is a breakthrough overload for a beginner and a trivial maintenance session for a veteran. Impact depends on what the body is already prepared for. A stimulus only produces adaptation if it exceeds what the athlete is accustomed to.

- **Individual response** — [FACT] Athletes are biologically heterogeneous. The same training produces different magnitudes of adaptation across individuals ("responders" and "non-responders" are documented). [UNKNOWN] the full set of factors that determine an individual's response.

- **Recovery context** — [FACT] Sleep, nutrition, life stress, age. The adaptation happens during recovery; two athletes with the same workout but different recovery experience different impact. [HYPOTHESIS] much of this is invisible to Aurora unless the athlete reports it.

- **Purpose** — [FACT] If the athletes are training toward different competitions, the *competitive* impact of an identical workout differs entirely.

- **Meaning** — [HYPOTHESIS] The same workout means different things to different athletes psychologically. Finishing a hard session is a confidence-defining event for one and routine for another. The mental impact is personal.

**The deepest version of the answer:** Impact is not a property of the workout. It is a property of the *encounter* between a specific workout and a specific athlete in a specific state with a specific history and a specific purpose. [ASSUMPTION] This is the foundational stance of Aurora's entire domain: **there is no such thing as the impact of a workout in general — only its impact on someone.**

This is why a database of workouts can never answer Aurora's question. The workout is necessary but never sufficient.

## Part 4 — Candidate domain language

Discovered, not decided. These are candidates for the ubiquitous language — to be validated, refined, or rejected. They are deliberately about *consequence*, since that is what was missing from the existing language (which is rich in event and state terms but thin on the relationship between them).

| Candidate term | Provisional meaning | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Impact** | The consequence of a workout on a specific athlete, across dimensions and timescales. | [QUESTION] core term — needs adoption |
| **Stimulus** | The demand a workout places on the athlete — the *input* to impact, distinct from the workout itself. | [HYPOTHESIS] useful |
| **Response** | How a specific athlete's body and mind answer a stimulus — the *mediating function*. | [HYPOTHESIS] useful |
| **Adaptation** | Lasting positive change produced when a stimulus is recovered from. | [FACT] established term |
| **Impact Maturation** | The way a workout's assessed impact changes as time reveals its delayed and accumulated effects. | [QUESTION] novel — does Aurora need this concept? |
| **Impact Signature** | The (possibly faint) trace a workout's impact leaves in observable data. | [HYPOTHESIS] useful for separating "had impact" from "impact we can see" |
| **Latent Impact** | Real impact that has occurred but is not yet observable. | [HYPOTHESIS] |
| **Encounter** | The meeting of a specific workout with a specific athlete-in-state; the unit at which impact is actually determined. | [QUESTION] possibly the true core concept |

**Note on existing language:** The current ubiquitous language defines `Training Load`, `Fatigue`, `Form`, `Performance` — these describe the athlete's *state*. This discovery suggests Aurora is missing vocabulary for the *process that connects a workout to a change in state*. That gap is the subject of this document.

## Part 5 — Open questions

Carried forward for resolution. These block model design.

1. **[QUESTION]** Should Aurora attempt to reason about technical and mental impact at all, given they are largely invisible to sensors — or scope to physical/competitive and let the athlete report the rest?
2. **[QUESTION]** If a workout's true impact only becomes knowable weeks later, what does Aurora say on the day of the workout? How does it represent a provisional judgment honestly?
3. **[QUESTION]** How does Aurora avoid systematically undervaluing quiet, accumulative workouts that have low immediate signal but high long-term impact?
4. **[QUESTION]** How much of "the athlete as mediator" can Aurora actually know? What is the minimum the athlete must tell us for impact inference to be meaningful?
5. **[QUESTION]** Is "impact" one thing with dimensions, or several genuinely independent things that should never be combined into a single judgment or score?
6. **[QUESTION]** Can mental impact be inferred from behavioral patterns over time (consistency, finishing, choosing hard options) without being measured in any single session?

---

## Part 6 — Unknowns

Stated plainly, because pretending to know is the one thing Aurora must never do.

- **[UNKNOWN]** The complete set of factors that determine an individual athlete's response to a given stimulus. Physiology has identified many; it has not closed the list.
- **[UNKNOWN]** Whether technical/movement quality is recoverable from the data Aurora has, or requires observation we don't possess.
- **[UNKNOWN]** Whether mental impact leaves any reliable trace in training data at all.
- **[UNKNOWN]** The lag structure of delayed impact for a *specific* athlete — supercompensation is real in general, but its timing is individual.
- **[UNKNOWN]** How much life context (sleep, stress, nutrition) we can obtain, and therefore how much of recovery — where adaptation actually happens — is observable.

---

## Part 7 — Hypotheses to carry forward

The load-bearing beliefs of this discovery. If these are wrong, the model built on them is wrong.

- **[HYPOTHESIS]** Impact can be usefully inferred from the workout plus a sufficiently rich model of the athlete, even though impact is never directly observed.
- **[HYPOTHESIS]** The athlete-as-mediator can be modeled well enough that inference beats the athlete's own intuition often enough to be worth trusting.
- **[HYPOTHESIS]** Delayed and accumulated impact can be confirmed in hindsight, allowing Aurora's understanding to improve over time.
- **[HYPOTHESIS]** Consistency (accumulated impact) predicts long-term improvement more strongly than any individual session's immediate impact.
- **[ASSUMPTION]** It is better for Aurora to say "I don't yet know this workout's impact" than to assign a confident number that is wrong. Honesty about latent impact is more valuable than false precision.

---

## Final reflection

> **If Aurora could only understand one thing about a workout, what should that be?**

Not its difficulty. Not its load. Not its numbers. Those describe the *event*, and the event is not what matters.

If Aurora could understand only one thing, it should understand:

**What this workout changed in *this* athlete — and whether that change moves them toward who they are trying to become.**

Everything else is in service of this. The dimensions (physical, technical, mental, competitive) are the *kinds* of change. The timescales (immediate, delayed, accumulated) are *when* the change becomes real. The athlete-as-mediator is *why* the same workout changes two people differently. But the one thing — the irreducible thing — is the change itself, understood relative to the athlete's direction.

A workout that changed nothing was, for Aurora's purposes, not a workout. It was just an event that happened.

A workout that moved the athlete — even invisibly, even in a way that won't show for months — is the only thing worth understanding.

Aurora studies that movement. Not the event that caused it.
